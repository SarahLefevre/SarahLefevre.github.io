Research

Before I make a painting, I do a little investigation about the topic. How was this subject handled before and how can I make it my own... The goal is to make paintings that are traditional in a way, but still have there own free touch.

When I first started painting, I never really though about what I wanted to make. I would see an image that grabbed my attention or spoke to me and then I would copy this with paint. I never knew this was a no-go untill I actually started to study art. When I went to art school, the teachers thought me that a painting should have a meaning. A true artist wants to say something with his work, make a difference in society. 
After I got this information, I thought to myself what my interests are and how I could create something special with this. I started doing research on how other painters or artists portrayed the things that I want to paint. I try to find out if they had different views or motifs. In many cases, having this new information can change the entire outcome of a painting.
