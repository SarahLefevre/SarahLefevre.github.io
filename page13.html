My inspiration

As an artist I have many influences. This means that I get inspired by objects, people, works of art and especially other artists. 

As you scroll down this page, you will find a list of painters that inspire me. These are artist that made me want to begin painting. I listed the reason why each artist specifically inspired me. Either because of the technical skills, the use of color or the material use of the paint itself.

This list is not in a chronological order of who inspired me first or who is most important to me personally. The artists are placed in a random order to prevent people from only reading the first five. They are all equally important and great. At the bottom of the page you can find a gallery of paintings these artists made.

  • Caravaggio - This man was a master in the art of painting. His 'clair obscur' work were one of the first paintings I ever saw as a child and I immediately fell in love with it. I also admire the technical quality of his work. The amount of detail that he achieves is amazing.
  • John Singer Sargent - What I like in the work of Sargent are the touches of color he uses. He will paint a work in darker tones and than add some light touches where the light hits the person or room he paints. This creates the illusion that his subject is alive.
  • Michaël Borremans - This is a contemporary painter who completely understands the technical side of painting. When you see the way he paints human flesh, you almost believe it is a photograph. But what makes his work special, is that he changes a few things in his image that make it almost surrealistic.
  • Edouard Manet - The first time I saw this work in real life I was completely blown away. His paintings look very realistic from far away, but when I looked close by it has such great feeling. I could spend houres watching his work. He paints with great freedom and still manages to make his painting look accomplished. This is definitely the painter I aspire to be one day.
  • Piere-Auguste Renoir - The work of this artist inspires me because of the colors. He uses a thousand colors in one painting and he still makes it look light and fresh. When you look at his work you can tell the different objects apart even if the colors run through the different objects and everything is linked. 
  • Joaquin Sorolla - The beach paintings Sorolla made are fantastic to me. You can feel the emotions his characters feel and are almost thrown into the painting itself. The background is bursting with colors and light. The sand and the sea also looks very realistic. It's like you can almost touch it.
  • Sir Lawrence Alma-Tadema - This artist became important to me when I studied Greek and Latin in school. There were always pictures of this artist in my school books. I spend my classes watching these paintings with great interest. I am inspired by the subject of Greek and Roman mythology and the amount of detail Tadema manages to paint. 
  • Pierre-Auguste Cot - The painting you can find on the bottom of this page is the first painting that ever caught my attention. It is a true masterpiece. The way he paints the nature and the surroundings caugt my eye. The simple and almost kitch subject would repell a lot of people but I find this attractive. An image can be just as beautiful if it is just a simple image. I still love it greately to this day.
  • Eric Fiscl - This painter inspired me greately when I first started painting. I wanted to learn how to paint the touches of light he created. His paint was thick and greasy which makes his work interesting. I studied it for a long time when I was learning. 
  • Edouard Vuillard - In the work of Vuillard it's not the amount of paint or color that drew me in, but the lack of it. It looks like he dragged his paint away and his colors are faded. Which gives it a sombre and dry look. Than he will add a hard, dark line of contour or white paint as light which makes these parts of the body or surrounding stand out.  
  • Jan De Vliegher - This modern painter does not care for detail or precision. He puts the paint on his painting in thick and direct lines. If it is not correct than that's just how it is. This shows his skill because you can tell what he paints the second you look at it. His use of color is also significant to his work and make it something you don't see every day.
  • Leonard Zorn - This artist reminds me a little bit of John Singer Sargent. It is painted in the same style, where they use dull, soft colors. Zorn will then put one bright color in his work, for example bright red, which makes this stand out and change the entire image.
