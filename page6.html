'Animals in Art'

Research

This page contains another theme I have recently worked with. I made a few paintings studying animals and how to suggest them. This proces helped my technical skills with a paintbrush and helped spread my conceptual ideas. I spend some time discovering how previous artists treated this subject. I made a convienent timeline of my findings a little further on. On the bottom of the page you can find a detailed explanation of my full process.

Representation of 'Animals in Art' in the past

Here you will find a chronological timeline of periods where animals were portrayed in art.
It doesn't show every art style that has excisted, only the ones where the perception of an animal changed in society causing the artform and representation to change with that. At the bottom of this list you will also find a few images that I found that give an idea of what art in that specific time looked like.
If you click the globe icons next to the discriptions, you will be directed to the source where I found some of my information.

Prehistoric Art

The first paintings were wall paintings made by caveman in this era. They painted the wild animals on which they hunted. These cave paintings were made as a kind of magic ritual during the hunt to overcome their fears and win during their battles. (As seen in the image below).

Greek / Hellenistic and Roman Art

In this time period there were a lot of fresco's and wall paintings on which animals were depicted. As you may know were Greek and Roman people polytheistic. They believed in different gods, each with their own mythology. Many of these gods were associated with animals. Because of this a lot of legends and stories arose where humans got animalistic character traits. E.g. the centaur. Animals were also depicted in pictures of their national sports. (As seen in the image below). 

The Middle Ages

These times were very fruitful for the topic 'animals'. Different genres were created when painting animals. There were portraits, historical pieces, landscapes, cityscapes, still lifes (these were done as a study object or decorative motif)... What often occurred is that animals were represented as a symbolic sign in a painting. Each animal had it's own meaning. The owl and the serpent were ominous, while the ape stood for vice and the dog for fidelity. (As seen in the image below).

Romanticism

This age went on with the conceptual idea of The Middel Ages. The paintings became more specific and detailed. The animal paintings were categorized into different topics such as: fur, animals as companion, scientific research, sport, entertainment, livestock farming, fishing, working animals, wild animals... Because of this specialized animal painters, better known als "Animaliers", emerged. Like Snyders, De Vos, Fyt and Stubbs. (As seen in the image below).

Surrealism

At this time the artists believed that logic alone was not sufficient, they turned to a dream world of the subconscious. They wanted to cross the boundaries of the unknown. Salvador Dali is the perfect example of this mindset. (As seen in the image below).

Modern Art

During this time animals are not only represented as just a being. There is another meaning behind it. Most artists involve animals in their work as an indication of our impure and corrupt society. The animal is a tool for the activists and critics. The most known example is De Bruyckere who uses installations of horses to draw the attention of the massas to the horrors of our current society or even previous events like WOII.
