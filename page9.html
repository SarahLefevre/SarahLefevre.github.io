'Children in Art'

Research

On this page you will find the first theme.
I have spend some time painting the behaviour en mimics of children. I put them in a surrounding that was strange to them and follow them around to study their peculiar reactions to things they have never seen before. I also spend some time discovering how previous artists treated this subject. I made a convienent timeline of my findings a little further on. On the bottom of the page you can find a detailed explanation of my full process. 

When I first started thinking about this topic, 

omdat ik lesgeef aan kinderen van die leeftijd breng ik er veel tijd mee door. ik heb al ontdekt dat alles voor hun opgeblazen wordt. ze vinden de meest onbenullige dingen spectaculair of absoluut onbenullig. bijvoorbeeld als iemand anders een snoepje heeft gekregen in een andere smaak, kan een kind van die leeftijd helemaal devastated zijn. dit intrigeerde mij heel erg. daarom wilde ik een soort 'nostalgie' reeks maken van werken waarvan de bedoeling is dat je in het werk getrokken wordt alsof je zelf nog een kind bent. of aan de andere kant dat je deze kinderen ziet en nadenkt over hoe onschuldig ze zijn. daarom koos ik er ook voor om meisjes te fotograferen en schilderen want dit heeft net nog een tikkeltje meer het soort effect dat ik wou bereiken.

Representation of 'Children in Art' in the past

Here you will find a chronological timeline of periods where children were portrayed in art.
It doesn't show every art style that has excisted, only the ones where the perception of a child changed in society causing the artform and representation to change with that. At the bottom of this list you will also find a few images that I found that give an idea of what art in that specific time looked like.
If you click the globe icons next to the discriptions, you will be directed to the source where I found some of my information.

Greek / Hellenistic and Roman Art

During this time Greek and Roman Peope were very serious about their beliefs, children were mostly portrayed with animals as a refrence to specific mythologies about their gods. The representation of their children is usually unrealistic, as they had very idealistic ideas about what a body, specifically a God's body, should look like. e.g. Boethus made several sculptures of children (as seen in the image below). 

The Middle Ages

The most popular subject in art in this time was religion, mostly because only churches were able to afford paintings. This means that in the Western culture, babies in paintings were usually a depiction of Jezus Christ. These babies had incorrect proportions and looked like miniature adults. This was entirely intended. It shows us how the society of that time looked at their children. e.g. Duccio depicted Jezus as a tiny old man in his painting 'The Madonna and Child' (as seen in the picture below).

Renaissance

During this period of time, the values had already changed a lot. The non-religious art flourished and now even the middle class could afford a painting. This lead to a different representation of children. There was a new belief that children were free of sin as they were born without knowledge. They started to look less creepy and a lot more realistic to please the common people. This realism was achieved by using reallife models instead of other paintings as a refrence.  

Baroque / Post-Renaissance

In this timeperiod, children were portrayed even more naive and realistic. New subjects, like domestic scenes, became much more popular. Although, the religious background did not disappear completely. Some artist like Murillo still painting with this theme (as seen in the picture below).

Romanticism

During this time, the figure of the child caused a lot of conflict. There was a great devide between the rich and the poor because of the economic issues. This caused society to reconsider what it meant to be a child. e.g. Jean-Jaques Rousseau recognized the child to have its own entity. This would later change the views on education, worklife and the way art depictioned children. 

Impressionism

This was the time of rebellion against the previous perfectionistic views on art. The new conception was about light, colour and life. Artist started to prefer working in open air, which meant that the children they would paint were real life models who ran around a garden or beach, while the artist tried to capture their essence and childlike spirit. Because these models did not have to sit still, the artists were unable the work precise. It is very noticable in their work. e.g. Potthast worked with thick touches to bring the painting to life (as seen in the picture below).

Surrealism

After WOII, people were in great need of some positive input. That is why surrealism artist mostely based their work on fantasies and a world of dreams. Some would say this comes close to escapism. The difference was that in surrealistic art, the artist wanted to express the belief that the values of society had become meaningless. The would take images from their mind and put them on paper in a sketch like manner, almost as if a child drew them. This is why children were again greatly researched, was the vision of a child more knowledgeable? e.g. The dreamy picture of a girl by Dali (as seen in the picture below).

Modern Art

In modern art, I have expierenced that there is overall less attention for children. We are more wrapped up in other social issues. There are ofcourse some artist who work with this topic, e.g. Ionut who lives in the world of Photoshop and digitially assembles surreal portraits of children with landscapes (as seen in the picture below). But I feel that we don't see enough work around children, while you can still make it an interesting subject.

At the beginning of my research I worked with a doll. My first purpose was the find the correct way to portray a childlike figure. That's why I used my doll. I would put her down underneath a light and study her. I made a few sketches and paintings to controle the right technique. The next step was making the colors and shape work on my paintings. I had to find a way to combine my doll or child with the background. To do this, I took a colorfull carpet with geometric shapes. I made these shapes my own and made a whole other image by constantly changing the perspectif. 

Later I took photographs of children running around and playing in an unfamiliar environment. I wanted to see how they reacted to there new surroundings. After a while they forget I was there following them around with a camera and started to talk freely to each other. It was a strange experience to see how a childs mind really works.
