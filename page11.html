About

I'm a young Belgian painter. My paintings are overall a mixture of
realisme and romanticism, with a hint of abstract art. My main focus
is to bring a picture to life that I see in my head and try to make it
realistic but still mysterious. I'll change a certain color or shape to
something that makes more sense in my head. I has to look like it could
vaguely be real whitout it being hypperrealistic. When you look at art,
you need to be able to see what it is supposed to be, without immediatly
knowing what it is really trying to say. The journey to finding that truth,
is what makes art beautiful.



My interest in art began when I was still a young girl. I think it just runs in my veins. My grandfather was a sculpture. I was always in his studio watching him carefully. He would show me how things worked and I would learn. Then I would take out a notebook and draw. This is a photograph of him doing the same thing with my aunt in 1966. She also works in the world of art now. I feel like I owe it to him to surround myself with creativeness. 


I'm currently studying arts in the well known School of Arts 'Sint-Luca' in Ghent. I've learned what the true meaning of art is. It's also extremly usefull to be around people who have different opinions on the matter. I learn a new view on art everyday, which is very usefull for my work. In the years I've been here, I've seen my work evolve and change in ways I couldn't have imagined if I wouldv'e studies something else.

I inspire to be an painter worthy of the name 'artist'. I try to expiriment as much as possible with my work. Different colors, different backgrounds, different styles even. I have a lot of painters who inspire my; Pieter Paul Rubens, Caravaggio, Renoir, Pierre Auguste Cot, Jan de Vliegher, Edouard Vuillard, John Singer Sargent...
I will always look at their work and try to incorporate their views in my work.
